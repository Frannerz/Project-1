Teach & Travel

Want to make the most of those long teacher holidays? On this page, you'll find some of my favourite places to visit for year-round sunshine, fun, wildlife and adventures. Trips are organised by time of year and you'll find links in each section to useful websites.

My Top Picks

Autumn

That first half term at school can be pretty exhausting! You could spend your holiday catching up on school work and lying on the sofa, but why not get away and forget about work for a week? Whether you'd prefer to stay in the UK or head further afield, there are some great options for you!

New York

  • When? October
  • Duration? 5-7 days
  • Activity level? Low/moderate

Visit New York in the Autumn (or fall) for the changing of seasons. Stroll through Central Park to see the beautiful fall colours, eat out in the West Village and catch an American Football game.

Don't miss...
  1. Catching a Giant's or a Knicks game
  2. Eating in the West Village
  3. Getting a great view of the city from Brooklyn
  4. Walking along the Skyline
  5. Cocktails in a rooftop bar

Mini-Break, UK

  • When? October
  • Duration? 3-6 days
  • Activity level? Low/moderate

Want to stay somewhere a bit closer to home this holiday? Why not spend a few days at a spa hotel or a shepherd's hut. I stayed in this lovely shepherd's hut in Essex for a few days last year, complete with outdoor bath and wood-burner. If you don't live near Essex, have a look on AirBNB for one near you!

Things to do on your mini-break...

  1. Go for a walk and a pub lunch
  2. Bathe in an outdoor bath or jacuzzi
  3. Play cards or board games
  4. Read a book

Winter

With at least 2 weeks off over the winter break, and another week off in February, there are plenty of destinations to choose from.

Ski the Three Valleys, France

  • When? December or February
  • Duration? 1 week
  • Activity level? Active

Visit Courchevel in the French Alps in December or February holidays. Here you'll have access to the largest ski area in the world, Les 3 Vall√©es (The Three Valleys).

As well as the 600 km of ski slopes, you'll find great Aprés bars, amazing restaurants (both in resort and on the mountain).

Tired from all that skiing and partying? Head to Aquamotion, a wellness centre equipped with indoor and outdoor pools, saunas, steam rooms, a spa and much more.


South Africa

  • When? December
  • Duration? 2+ weeks
  • Activity level? Low/moderate

For some winter sun, visit South Africa. Take a ride on the wine tram in the Franschhoek Valley, spot the big 5 in Kruger Safari Park and visit world-class beaches, South Africa has something for everyone. There is so much to do, you won't fit it all on one trip.

For wildlife...
Visit Kruger Safari Park or a private game reserve. Visit Boulders' Beach to see wild African penguins.
For gastronomy...
Visit Cape Town, voted by Condé Nast's readers as the best food city in the world.
For scenery...
Visit The Garden Route where you'll find some of the best beaches.

Spring

Skiing is still a great option in Spring; the sun will be out, the snow will be softer and you'll be swapping vin chaude for rosé. But if you fancy swapping your salopettes for swimwear, there are lots of other great choices.

Mauritius

  • When? April
  • Duration? 2 weeks
  • Activity level? Low/moderate

Visit Mauritius for diverse wildlife, breathtaking scenery, white sandy beaches and delicious cuisine. You can find cheaper stays as well as all-inclusive resorts, if you're not looking to break the bank! Expect hot weather at this time of year. If you prefer it a bit cooler, visit in August. The island is quite large, so I recommend hiring a car to maximise your exploring potential.

Don't miss...
  1. Dolphin and whale watching
  2. Eating cheaply at a Table d'hote or a food van by the beach
  3. Watching the sunset on the beach
  4. Going on a waterfall hike and catching some incredible views
  5. Having lunch at a panoramic restaurant

Japan

  • When? April
  • Duration? 2 weeks
  • Activity level? Low/Moderate

Visit Japan in the spring the catch a glimpse of the beautiful cherry blossoms. While you are there, take in breath-taking views of Mount Fuji from the the Fuji Five Lake Region. If you are lucky, you may catch a glimpse of the famous shibazakura (pink moss).

Other must-sees include Sumo wrestling (if it is on while you are there), themed restaurants and bars in Tokyo, the Fushimi Inari Shrine in Kyoto and the various local cuisines of each region. A visit to an onsen is also a must-do (be prepared for some nudity)!

My favourite Japanese dishes:

  • Okonomiyaki (best tried in Hiroshima)
  • Sushi
  • Tonkatsu
  • Ramen

Summer

For travel in the summer, I like to go a little further afield. My favourite place to go is central America. Word of warning: it is hurricane season at this time but not in every region. Keep an eye on weather warnings while you are there and take advice from locals. warnings aside, if you are looking for adventure, nature, getting off the beaten track, history, local flavour... (I could go on!), then Central America is a great choice.

Make the most of your trip and visit at least 2 countries while you are there.The flights can be expensive, but it's relatively cheap once you get there.

Guatemala

  • When? July and August (you can also go in December or March)
  • Duration? 2 weeks
  • Activity level? Active

Guatemala is a country of stunning natural beauty, history, and culture. It also has a great party scene! The weather is warm in the summer but expect some rain, too. There are some beautiful colonial cities as well as Ancient Mayan ruins to visit during your stay.

Don't miss...
  1. The Mayan City of Tikal at sunrise
  2. Sumac Champey and river rafting
  3. A volcano hike from Antigua
  4. The pool party in Antigua

Nicaragua

  • When? July and August (you can also go in December or March)
  • Duration? 2+ weeks
  • Activity level? Moderate

My trip to Nicaragua was one of the best I have been on. I visited in 2019 when they were just recovering from a lot of civil unrest. There were not many tourists around so you really felt like we were off the beaten track. There is everything there, from rainforests, to volcanoes, to caribbean islands.

See some colonial architecture and hike a volcano in Leon... go canoeing or climb a volcano on the island of Omotepe... eat lobster and bathe in crystal clear waters on the Corn Islands... or surf and party in San Juan; Nicaragua has it all.

Don't miss...

  1. A visit the Corn Islands
  2. Canoeing on Omotepe
  3. A luxury stay in San Juan or Little Corn Island
  4. A walk in the rainforest
